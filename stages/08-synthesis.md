# Stage 08 — Validation Scorecard & Synthesis

## Purpose

Synthesize all prior stages into a single go/no-go/pivot recommendation. Rate confidence across every dimension, surface the overall assessment, and produce a prioritized action plan. The executive summary is written LAST — it is the distillation of everything, not a preview.

---

## Discovery Mode

Present the scorecard and recommendation, then discuss:

1. "Here is where we landed across all dimensions. [Present scorecard table.] Any of these surprise you or feel off?"
2. "My overall assessment is [Go/Conditional Go/Pivot/No-Go]. Here is why: [1-2 sentence rationale]. Does that match your gut feeling?"
3. "The most important next steps are [top 2-3 from gap analysis]. Do you want to walk through the validation plans for those?"
4. "Is there anything we didn't cover that feels important to your decision?"

**Adapt based on assessment:**
- **Go**: Focus on execution priorities and timeline
- **Conditional Go**: Focus on which gaps to close first and what would change the assessment
- **Pivot**: Explore what is salvageable — the validated problem, the market insight, the team capability
- **No-Go**: Acknowledge clearly, highlight what was learned, suggest adjacent opportunities if any emerged

---

## Draft Mode

Execute this synthesis process in order:

### Step 1: Score Each Dimension

For each stage, assign a confidence rating based on the evidence quality and gap analysis from Stage 07:

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **High** | Primary evidence supports key findings. Critical assumptions validated or have strong secondary evidence. |
| **Medium** | Mix of secondary evidence and reasonable assumptions. Critical assumptions identified with validation plans. |
| **Low** | Mostly assumptions or weak secondary evidence. Critical gaps remain without clear validation path. |

### Step 2: Determine Overall Assessment

Apply this decision logic:

- **Go**: 4-5 dimensions rated High, no Critical assumptions unvalidated. Proceed to build/launch.
- **Conditional Go**: 2-3 dimensions rated High, remaining are Medium. Critical assumptions have validation plans. Proceed but execute validation plans in parallel with building.
- **Pivot**: Problem and/or Market rated High, but Solution and/or Business Model rated Low. The problem is real but the approach needs rethinking.
- **No-Go**: Problem rated Low, OR Market rated Low with no viable segment identified. Fundamental premise is not validated.

Use judgment — these are guidelines, not rigid rules. A single Low-confidence Critical assumption can override an otherwise strong scorecard.

### Step 3: Build Next Steps

Pull from Stage 07's validation roadmap and add execution steps:

1. **Immediate** (this week): Highest-priority validation activity from Stage 07
2. **Short-term** (2-4 weeks): Remaining critical validations, MVP scoping decisions
3. **Medium-term** (1-3 months): Build/test cycle based on validation results

For Pivot or No-Go assessments, next steps focus on:
- What to preserve (insights, contacts, research)
- Adjacent opportunities worth exploring
- Whether to re-enter at a different angle

### Step 4: Write Executive Summary

Write this LAST. It synthesizes the entire report into 4-6 sentences covering:
- What idea was validated and for whom
- The strongest signal (what we are most confident about)
- The biggest risk (what we are least confident about)
- The recommendation and primary next step

---

## Gate Criteria

This is the final stage. The report is complete when:
- [ ] All 5 dimensions are scored with rationale
- [ ] Overall assessment is stated with clear reasoning
- [ ] Next steps are prioritized and actionable (not generic)
- [ ] Executive summary is written and accurately reflects the full analysis
- [ ] User has reviewed and acknowledged the findings (discovery mode)

---

## Confidence Scoring

The scorecard IS the confidence scoring for this stage. No separate assessment needed.
